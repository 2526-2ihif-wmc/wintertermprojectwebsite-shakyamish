Abyssinian

The Abyssinian has a friendly and outgoing personality, so they love to play and hate being alone. While this medium-sized cat is very social, their adaptable temperament and playful nature allow them to find fun in tackling toys or racing up and down stairs (or scratching posts) all by themselves. Soon enough, though, an Aby will seek attention from their housemates, no matter the species. Full-grown around a year of age, the Abyssinian will maintain a kitten-like quality well into adulthood despite their elegant, refined appearance. Abyssinians are known as “Aby-grabbys” because they like to grab things and knock them off counters. Also, these cats are known as the “clowns of the cat kingdom,” and are even referred to as “Aby-silly-an.” Their silly, playful nature helps them get along great with dogs.

Aegean

The Aegean cat has a friendly, confident personality and thrives on companionship, making them happiest when they're part of everyday household life. Social and intelligent, they enjoy interactive play and will gladly follow their people from room to room, curious about everything that's going on. While they can entertain themselves for a while, Aegeans prefer having company and aren't fans of being ignored for too long. Reaching full maturity around one year of age, the Aegean cat retains a playful, youthful spirit well into adulthood. Agile and athletic, they love climbing, chasing, and exploring, often with a surprisingly strong fascination for water. Known for their talkative nature and expressive behaviors, these cats aren't shy about asking for attention. Their easygoing, affectionate temperament helps them get along well with children, other cats, and even dogs, making them a natural fit for lively, social homes.

American Bobtail

The American Bobtail is an affectionate, easygoing cat with a confident yet gentle personality. Known for forming strong bonds with their people, these cats enjoy being involved in family life without demanding constant attention. Playful and intelligent, American Bobtails love interactive games and puzzle toys, but they're also content entertaining themselves when needed. Maturing a bit more slowly than some breeds, the American Bobtail keeps a kitten-like curiosity well into adulthood. Their naturally short, expressive tail adds to their charm and often reflects their mood. Sometimes described as “dog-like,” Bobtails may follow their humans around, greet them at the door, or even enjoy leash training. With their adaptable nature and friendly demeanor, they tend to get along well with children, other cats, and dogs, making them excellent companions for a wide variety of households.

American Curl

The American Curl is a people-loving, affectionate cat with a sweet and adaptable personality. Curious and playful, they enjoy being part of whatever their humans are doing and will often perch nearby to observe or “help.” While they love interactive play and attention, American Curls aren't overly demanding and balance their social nature with a calm, gentle demeanor. Slow to fully mature, the American Curl keeps a kitten-like enthusiasm well into adulthood. Their signature backward-curled ears give them a perpetually surprised expression that perfectly matches their lively, inquisitive spirit. Often described as friendly and polite, these cats tend to greet guests warmly and get along well with children, other cats, and dogs. With their affectionate nature and easygoing charm, American Curls make wonderful companions for families and individuals alike.

American Ringtail

The American Ringtail is an affectionate, people-focused cat with a lively and engaging personality. Highly social and intelligent, they enjoy interacting with their humans and often choose to stay close, whether that means following them around the house or curling up nearby. Playful and curious, Ringtails love interactive toys and games but are also happy to entertain themselves when their people are busy. Slow to mature, the American Ringtail keeps a youthful, energetic spirit well into adulthood. Their defining feature—the tail that naturally curls over their back when they're relaxed or happy—adds to their expressive charm. Sometimes described as “dog-like” in their loyalty, these cats tend to form strong bonds and may greet their humans at the door. With their adaptable, friendly nature, American Ringtails generally get along well with children, other cats, and dogs, making them a great fit for active, social households.

American Shorthair

The American Shorthair is a friendly, easygoing cat with a balanced and adaptable personality. Known for their gentle nature, they enjoy spending time with their humans without being overly demanding, making them a perfect fit for families and busy households alike. Playful and curious, they love toys, games, and exploring their environment, yet they're equally content lounging nearby or observing the action from a cozy perch. Maturing around one year of age, American Shorthairs retain a healthy, energetic spirit well into adulthood. Their sturdy, muscular build and classic round face give them a timeless, approachable appearance, while their calm and sociable temperament helps them get along well with children, other cats, and even dogs. With a mix of playful charm and dependable companionship, American Shorthairs are the quintessential all-around family cat.

American Wirehair

The American Wirehair is a friendly, affectionate cat with a playful and curious personality. Known for their intelligence and adaptability, they enjoy interactive play and exploring their surroundings, yet they're equally content curling up with their humans for quiet companionship. Their social nature makes them eager to participate in family life without being overly demanding. Maturing around one year of age, American Wirehairs retain a lively, kitten-like spirit well into adulthood. Their signature wiry, springy coat gives them a distinctive texture and adds to their charming, unique appearance. Calm, easygoing, and gentle, they typically get along well with children, other cats, and dogs. With their playful curiosity and warm, adaptable temperament, American Wirehairs make affectionate and entertaining companions for a wide variety of households.

Aphrodite

The Aphrodite cat is a big, affectionate feline with a loyal, people-oriented personality that makes them feel like a true member of the family. These cats enjoy interactive play and will happily join you for adventures around the house, yet they also have a calm, gentle side and love curling up with their humans for cuddles. While they can entertain themselves, Aphrodite cats are happiest with company and don't like being left alone for long. Aphrodites mature more slowly than many breeds and tend to keep a youthful, playful quality well into adulthood. Their large, muscular build reflects their natural heritage—descended from hardy island cats of Cyprus—yet despite their size they're known for their easygoing, affectionate temperament. Often described as almost “dog-like” in loyalty and social engagement, they'll follow you from room to room and are quick to show affection. With intelligence and charm to spare, Aphrodite cats generally get along well with children, other cats, and dogs, making them wonderful companions for active, loving homes.

Arabian Mau

The Arabian Mau is a friendly, active cat with a strong, adaptable personality shaped by its natural desert environment. Highly intelligent and curious, they enjoy exploring their surroundings, climbing, and engaging in interactive play, yet they are equally content relaxing nearby with their humans. Arabian Maus form strong bonds with their families and thrive on companionship, making them affectionate without being overly demanding. Maturing around one year of age, Arabian Maus retain a lively, playful spirit well into adulthood. Their sleek, muscular build and short, dense coat reflect their natural origins, giving them agility and endurance. Known for their gentle, social nature, they usually get along well with children, other cats, and dogs. With a mix of energy, intelligence, and devotion, Arabian Maus make charming, adaptable companions for households that enjoy active, engaged pets.

Asian

The Asian cat is a friendly, social feline with a playful and affectionate personality. Curious and intelligent, they enjoy interactive games, exploring their surroundings, and keeping busy, yet they're equally happy to curl up with their humans for quiet companionship. Asian cats thrive on attention and love being involved in family life, making them highly people-oriented without being demanding. Maturing around one year of age, Asian cats retain a youthful, lively spirit well into adulthood. Their sleek, muscular build and striking coat patterns—ranging from solid colors to tabby and shaded varieties—give them a refined and elegant appearance. Known for their adaptable, gentle temperament, Asian cats usually get along well with children, other cats, and dogs. With a perfect balance of playful energy and affectionate loyalty, they make devoted, engaging companions for a wide variety of households.

Highly people-oriented, they thrive on companionship without being overly demanding. Australian Mist

The Australian Mist is a friendly, outgoing cat with a gentle and affectionate personality. Intelligent and curious, they enjoy interactive play, exploring their surroundings, and joining in family activities, yet they're equally happy to relax nearby and enjoy quiet companionship. Highly social, they thrive on attention and love being involved in household life without being overly demanding. Maturing around one year of age, the Australian Mist retains a playful, kitten-like spirit well into adulthood. Known for their adaptable, calm temperament, these cats usually get along well with children, other cats, and dogs. With their combination of affectionate charm and lively curiosity, Australian Mists make devoted, entertaining companions for a wide variety of households.
